AIAA D/B/F 2017

What is AIAA ?

The AIAA through the Applied Aerodynamics, Aircraft Design, Design Engineering and Flight Test Technical Committees and the AIAA Foundation invites all university students to participate in the Design/Build/Fly Competition. Major corporate sponsors for the competition are Textron Aviation, Raytheon Missile Systems, and AIAA Foundation. The contest will provide a real-world aircraft design experience for engineering students by giving them the opportunity to validate their analytic studies.

Student teams will design, fabricate, and demonstrate the flight capabilities of an unmanned, electric powered, radio controlled aircraft that can best meet the specified mission profile. The goal is a balanced design possessing good demonstrated flight handling qualities and practical and affordable manufacturing requirements while providing a high vehicle performance.

Problem Statement
Year 2017, the theme was “Tube-Launched UAV”, and required the aircraft to be designed such that all its features may be moved or rotated using mechanisms so as to be stowed inside a cylindrical tube of specific dimensions.
The aircraft must complete three missions in sequence and a ground mission.

Mission 1: Demonstration Flight, 3 laps of the field in 5 minutes.
Mission 2: Speed Flight, 3 laps carrying three regulation hockey pucks on board as payload
Mission 3: Range Flight, team specified quantity of hockey pucks to be carried, and number of laps flown in 5 minutes recorded.

Ground Mission: Designing the sealed launch tube and the UAV within it to withstand impact of a series of drops in different orientations, without suffering visible damage.
View the rules here : http://www.aiaadbf.org/Rules/
The fly off was held at Tucson, Arizona from 20th to 23rd April, 2017. A mandatory proposal was sent in October 2016 for qualification purposes.
The competition




The hard work and preparation that Aero VJTI put in for the competition finally paid off. Team Vayuputras finished with the top design report score out of 95 teams and secured the 7th rank overall.

In the process, several firsts were recorded. We became the first Asian team to finish on top in the report phase of the D/B/F. The top 10 listing was the best finish by an Asian team in the competition’s 20 years history which is historically dominated by American university teams.
